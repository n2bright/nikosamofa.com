02 — The Climb

The arc, in plain English

I started in IT at the Helpdesk. Got curious about cybersecurity — not the Hollywood version, the boring institutional version. The version where someone has to actually read the policy, audit the configuration, and tell the partner that yes, this needs to change.

I stacked the certifications people actually respect (Security+, CySA+, PenTest+). I went to work for one of the largest law firms in the world, running vendor risk reviews on the AI platforms attorneys are starting to use, governing access across enterprise SaaS, and operating Microsoft 365 security at scale.

And I noticed something. The work I do for a global firm with a budget — small law firms in my own backyard can't afford. They run unpatched tenants, no MFA on shared mailboxes, legacy IMAP wide open. ABA Rule 1.6 says they have to take "reasonable measures." Most of them don't know what that means.

So I started Watusoft. Five-to-ten-day engagements, productized pricing, plain-English reports. No upsells. No vendor kickbacks. The work I do at the firm, sized for the firms that need it most.

Master's degree finishing. CISM after that. Documenting all of it publicly — not because I'm trying to be an influencer, but because the climb itself is the asset.

"Document the climb, narrowly and consistently, until the climb itself becomes the brand."
